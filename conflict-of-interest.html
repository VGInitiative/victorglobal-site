Integrity & Accountability

Conflict of Interest Policy

Effective Date: January 1, 2026

Integrity is the bedrock of our airframe. The purpose of this policy is to protect the interests of Victor Global Initiative (VGI) when contemplating arrangements that might benefit the private interests of an officer or director[cite: 5].

1. Purpose & Scope

This policy ensures VGI avoids "excess benefit transactions" and serves as a supplement to applicable state and federal laws governing nonprofit organizations[cite: 5, 6].

2. Defining Interested Persons

An "Interested Person" is any director, principal officer, or committee member with board-delegated powers who has a direct or indirect financial interest[cite: 8]. Financial interest includes[cite: 9]:

  • Ownership or investment interests in entities VGI does business with[cite: 11].
  • Compensation arrangements with VGI or its affiliates[cite: 12].
  • Potential investments or compensation with entities VGI is currently negotiating with[cite: 14].

Compensation includes remuneration, gifts, or favors that are not insubstantial[cite: 15]. A financial interest is not an automatic conflict; the board determines if a conflict exists[cite: 16, 17].

3. Operating Procedures

In the event of a potential conflict, VGI follows a disciplined protocol[cite: 18]:

  • Duty to Disclose: Interested persons must disclose all material facts to the board or relevant committee[cite: 19].
  • Determination: The interested person must leave the meeting while the remaining board members discuss and vote on whether a conflict exists[cite: 20, 21].
  • Addressing Conflicts: The board will investigate alternatives to the proposed transaction to obtain a more advantageous arrangement that does not produce a conflict[cite: 25, 26].
  • Voting: If an alternative is not reasonably possible, the disinterested directors must determine by majority vote if the transaction is fair, reasonable, and in the Organization's best interest[cite: 27].

4. Compensation Restrictions

To ensure total transparency, any voting member of the governing board or compensation-related committees who receives compensation from VGI is precluded from voting on their own compensation matters[cite: 37, 38]. They may, however, provide information to committees regarding compensation[cite: 41].

5. Records & Reviews

VGI maintains rigorous records of all proceedings, including names of those present, the nature of the financial interest disclosed, and the final decision reached by the board[cite: 33, 34, 35]. To protect our tax-exempt status, we conduct periodic reviews of[cite: 48, 49]:

  • Reasonableness of compensation based on competent survey information[cite: 52].
  • Partnerships and joint ventures to ensure they further charitable purposes and reflect reasonable payments for goods and services[cite: 53].

6. Annual Affirmation

Every director, officer, and governing committee member is required to annually sign a statement affirming they have received, read, understood, and agreed to comply with this policy[cite: 42, 43, 44, 45, 46].
